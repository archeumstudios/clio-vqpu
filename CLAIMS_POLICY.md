# Public Claims Policy

## Rule

Every public claim must be supported by inspected implementation or retained experimental evidence. Planned features must be labeled as planned internally and must not appear as available in public-facing material.

## Defensible after implementation evidence exists

- Clio is a newly designed programmable virtual quantum processing architecture developed by Archeum Studios.
- Clio defines its own ISA, runtime, processor state, reference engine, tracing, validation, SDK, CLI, and desktop environment.
- Clio supports writing, executing, inspecting, debugging, validating, and studying hybrid quantum programs through a software-defined VQPU.
- Clio's built-in engine performs classical simulation of quantum-state evolution.

These statements are targets until their complete implementations and tests exist. Repository prose must distinguish architecture specifications from supported functionality.

## Forbidden

Do not claim that Clio is the first VQPU, physical quantum hardware, a quantum computer inside a laptop, evidence of quantum advantage or supremacy, an unlimited-qubit simulator, the fastest simulator, a replacement for physical QPUs or established frameworks, a fault-tolerant system, or a result source for experiments that were not run.

Do not use “real quantum computer on your laptop.” Prefer: “Program and inspect a software-defined VQPU whose built-in engine simulates quantum-state evolution on classical hardware.”

## Novelty

Novelty claims require a recorded primary-source literature review and must name the precise architectural or integrated-system distinction. Overlapping prior work must be cited fairly. The existence of simulators, quantum ISAs, tracing, or debugging is not itself claimed as novel.

## Quantitative claims

Benchmark and correctness statements must identify workload, configuration, hardware, software revision, sample/repetition method, uncertainty or tolerance, and retained source data. Do not hide negative results or compare incompatible configurations.

## Release review

Before publication, claims receive implementation, literature, scientific, legal, and product audits. Unverified superlatives and future-tense promises are removed.
